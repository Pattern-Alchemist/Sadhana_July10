# Preta-Siddhi — Field Manual

> A complete field manual for preta-saṁvāda (spirit communication) drawn from Aghora and Kaula tantric sources. Includes preparatory śuddhi rites, the Preta-Sandhi Yantra with geometric proportions, the Chakra-Maṇḍala layout, the seven diagnostic Prashna sequence, and the Preta-Mukti Kriyā (liberation rite).
>
> **Caution level: HIGH.** This text describes rites of spirit-contact and severance. It is preserved here as scholarly source material. It is **not** instructional for unsupervised practice. The rites involve risk to the practitioner's psychological and somatic stability if undertaken without adequate preparation, lineage grounding, and supervision.

---

## I. Domain Overview

Pret-Siddhi is a domain where the veil between the bhūta-loka (realm of spirits) and the manuṣya-loka (human realm) is held to be thinnest. To speak with a preta (a restless spirit caught between realms) is, in this tradition, not mere curiosity — it is a karmic engagement.

Such beings are described as rarely free-willed; they are bound by ṛṇa (unpaid debts), dveṣa (hatred), or moha-āndha (blind attachment). Before any contact is attempted, three truths must be understood:

- **Danger.** The preta's suffering is described as infectious. The practitioner's prāṇa and manas can be weakened if not anchored in śuddhi (purity) and bhaya-rahita citta (fearless mind).
- **Purpose.** The only legitimate purpose is to resolve, release, or protect — never to command for personal gain.
- **Protection.** Without a protective mantle, spirits may follow the practitioner or transfer their kleśa (affliction).

> **Editorial note.** The mantras below are reproduced as supplied. Several contain apparent transmission artifacts (e.g. "प्रकािय" for "प्रकाशय", "सक्न्ि" for "सन्धि", "बन्िनम्स्वाहा" for "बन्धनं स्वाहा"). The corrected forms are noted in editorial brackets where unambiguous. The source text is preserved verbatim for scholarly fidelity.

## II. Preparatory Śuddhi and Protection

Before any contact, three nights of bhūta-śuddhi are required.

### 1. Agnī-jāla Rakṣā (Fire-Net Protection)

a. Sit facing east at dawn.
b. Light seven sesame-oil lamps in a circle before you.
c. Chant 21 times:

> ॐ अग्ने जालसमन्तं मां रक्ष रक्ष स्वाहा
> *Om Agne Jālasamantaṁ Māṁ Rakṣa Rakṣa Svāhā*
> (O Fire, weave a net around me, protect me from all directions.)

### 2. Tripuṇḍra and Rakṣā-Sūtra

a. Apply sacred ash (vibhūti) in three lines across the forehead, chanting ॐ नमः शिवाय (*Om Namaḥ Śivāya*) thrice.
b. Tie a red rakṣā-sūtra (protection thread) on the right wrist, invoking the protection of one's iṣṭa-deva (chosen deity).

### 3. Dietary Restraint

a. No meat, alcohol, onions, or garlic.
b. One meal before sunset; avoid all food after.

## III. The Preta-Saṁvāda Siddhi-Vidhi

**Best time.** Kṛṣṇa Pakṣa (waning moon), midnight, 11:45 PM – 1:15 AM (Nishitha Kāla).
**Place.** The site where manifestations occur most often.

### 1. Ground Preparation

a. Draw a śaṭkoṇa (six-pointed star) with rice flour.
b. In the center, place a copper plate with:
   - Black sesame seeds
   - A dīpa (lamp) with mustard oil
   - A small water-filled kalaśa with mango leaves and coconut

### 2. Invocation Mantra

a. Sit facing north.
b. Chant 108 times on a rudrākṣa mālā:

> ॐ प्रेतात्मने आवाहयाशम सत्यं प्रकािय प्रकािय स्वाहा
> *[Om Pretātmane Āvāhayāmi Satyaṁ Prakāśaya Prakāśaya Svāhā]*
> (O spirit, I invoke you — reveal the truth, reveal it clearly.)

### 3. Spirit Recognition

a. Sudden cold, flame flicker, or breath heaviness signal the preta's approach.
b. Speak gently, using the name if known:

> "Āyāsa na karo — satya kahna hai, tumhe mukti milegi."
> (Do not struggle — speak the truth, you will be freed.)

### 4. Dialogue Method

a. Ask only direct questions: "Why are you here?", "Whom do you seek?", "What binds you?"
b. If the response comes through movement (lamp flicker, object shift, sound), acknowledge respectfully.

### 5. Release

a. After answers are received, chant 11 times:

> ॐ नमो भगवते रुद्ररूपिणे प्रतमक्ततदाय स्वाहा
> *[Om Namo Bhagavate Rudra-Rūpiṇe Preta-Mukti-Dāya Svāhā]*
> (Salutations to Rudra, granter of freedom to spirits.)

b. Offer water mixed with black sesame toward the south.

### ⚠ Caution

If during contact the heart rate spikes uncontrollably, vision blurs, or overwhelming grief enters — stop immediately, recite the Hanumān Cālīsā aloud, and dismiss the spirit.

## IV. The Preta-Sandhi Yantra

This yantra — the seal of truce between the human seeker and the preta — is used by Aghora and Kaula tantrics when they need to question a spirit without inviting harm. Its purpose is not to trap, but to hold the preta in a śānta-avasthā (non-aggressive state) while the dialogue takes place.

### IV.1 Yantra Structure

```
       ॐ
     / ↑ \
   ⬒  ○ ○  ⬓
     \ ↓ /
   ⬔  ○ ○  ⬕
       ह्रं
```

- **Outer shape.** Square with four gates (open yet protected communication).
- **Inner core.** Six-pointed star (śaṭkoṇa — balance between human and spirit realms).
- **Bindu center.** Small black dot (the preta's anchoring point during contact).

### IV.2 Construction Steps

**Best time.** 8th, 10th, or 14th lunar day of Kṛṣṇa Pakṣa, just before midnight.

**Materials.**
- Bhojpatra leaf or clean white cotton cloth
- Black ink from lamp soot + mustard oil
- Copper or bronze stylus (avoid iron — it agitates spirits)

**Method.**
1. Sit facing south.
2. Place a mustard-oil lamp to your left, and a small bowl of water with black sesame to your right.
3. Inhale deeply thrice, mentally chanting: ॐ क्ष्म्प्रेताय नमः (*Om Kṣm Pretāya Namaḥ*).
4. Draw the outer square with four openings at the cardinal points.
5. Draw the śaṭkoṇa inside — two triangles intersecting.
6. Place a black bindu at the exact center.
7. In each outer gate, write:
   - East: ॐ
   - South: ह्रं
   - West: क्षौं
   - North: फट्
8. Around the whole yantra, in a circular path, inscribe:

> ॐ प्रेत-सक्न्ि-कराय ह्रं क्षौं फट् स्वाहा
> *[Om Preta-Sandhi-Karāya Hrīṁ Kṣauṁ Phaṭ Svāhā]*
> (Om, for the making of spirit-contact, Hrīṁ Kṣauṁ Phaṭ Svāhā.)

### IV.3 Activation Ritual (Prāṇa-Pratiṣṭhā)

1. Hold the yantra between your palms at heart level.
2. Chant the above mantra 108 times while focusing on the intent: *"May this be the space of truth and peace between me and the spirit."*
3. Pass the yantra three times over the flame, three times over incense smoke, and three times over sesame water.
4. Place it flat before you during the Preta-Saṁvāda Vidhi, positioned between you and the expected point of manifestation.

### IV.4 Safety Protocol

- Once questioning is over, immerse the yantra in running water (river or stream). If no running water is available, bury it under a healthy peepal tree with black sesame and milk.
- Never reuse a preta-sandhi yantra — each is for a single engagement only.
- If the spirit becomes hostile despite the yantra, immediately chant ॐ क्षौं तलरं ह फट् (*[Om Kṣauṁ Tāḷaya Hūṁ Phaṭ]*) in a loud, commanding voice and extinguish the lamp.

## V. Geometric Proportions of the Preta-Sandhi Yantra

### V.1 Proportional Layout

The yantra is constructed inside a 12×12 unit grid for reproducibility on bhojpatra or white cloth without distortion.

- **Base square.** 12 × 12 unit grid.
- **Four gates.** Each gate is 2 units wide, centered on each side.
- **Śaṭkoṇa (star).** Outer triangle tips touch the midpoint of each side of the square's inner frame.
- **Bindu (center dot).** Radius = 0.2 units.

### V.2 Step-by-Step Construction

1. **Outer bhūpura.** Draw a square of 12 × 12 units. At the center of each side, leave a 2-unit opening — these are the gates. Line thickness: 0.2 units.
2. **Inner śaṭkoṇa.** Draw an equilateral upward triangle so each tip touches the inner edge of the square. Draw a second equilateral downward triangle inverted over it. The intersection forms a perfect hexagon at the center.
3. **Bindu.** In the very center of the hexagon, place a solid black dot of radius 0.2 units. This is where the preta's presence is anchored.
4. **Gate bīja placement.** Inside each gate opening, written toward the center:
   - East: ॐ
   - South: ह्रं
   - West: क्षौं
   - North: फट्
5. **Outer circular mantra ring.** Draw a circle passing through the outer corners of the gates. Around this circle, inscribe clockwise from the East gate:

> ॐ प्रेतसक्न्िकराय ह्रं क्षौं फट् स्वाहा
> *[Om Preta-Sandhi-Karāya Hrīṁ Kṣauṁ Phaṭ Svāhā]*

Ensure even spacing — letters must not touch.

### V.3 Symbolic Sketch

```
 ॐ───────────ह्रं
 │   ▲   ▲     │
 │  / \ / \    │
 │  /   X   \  │
फट् │  \ / \ /  │  क्षौं
 │   ▼   ▼     │
 │────────────│
```

*Note: the above is symbolic — the actual drawing should be geometric perfection.*

### V.4 Activation Reminder

1. Draw only during Kṛṣṇa Pakṣa, preferably on Aṣṭamī, Daśamī, or Chaturdaśī tithi.
2. Use lamp-soot ink mixed with mustard oil for preta work.
3. Activate with 108 chants of ॐ प्रेतसक्न्िकराय ह्रं क्षौं फट् स्वाहा.
4. Never keep the yantra after the rite — dissolve or bury as instructed.

## VI. The Preta-Sandhi Chakra-Maṇḍala

This is the full field arrangement used by adept tantrics so the preta's presence is bound within the dialogue space and cannot cross into the practitioner's personal prāṇa-kṣetra. It is the final operational layer that makes contact sharp, short, and safe.

### VI.1 Time, Place, Mental State

**Time.** Kṛṣṇa Pakṣa (waning moon), tithis Aṣṭamī, Daśamī, or Chaturdaśī. Muhūrta: 11:45 PM – 1:15 AM (Nishitha Kāla).

**Place.** Either at the main manifestation spot in the home, or at an isolated location where disturbance is minimal. The floor must be cleaned with gomūtra (cow urine) or salt water before setup.

**Mental state.** Absolute clarity of purpose: to identify cause, not to harm or control. Mind anchored in maitrī (benevolence) and abheda-bhāva (non-separation). Fearlessness is crucial.

### VI.2 Chakra-Maṇḍala Layout

Three concentric zones:

**1. Inner Zone — Yantra Core**
- Place the Preta-Sandhi Yantra at the exact center.
- On top of the yantra, keep a copper plate with:
  - A small mustard-oil dīpa (single wick, steady flame)
  - Seven black sesame seeds arranged around the lamp in a ring

**2. Middle Zone — Elemental Guard Ring**

This ring shields the practitioner and channels the preta's presence into the yantra. Position at exactly two arm-lengths radius from the yantra.

- **North.** Copper bowl of clean water with five tulsi leaves.
- **East.** Small clay pot with smoldering frankincense (lobān).
- **South.** Iron bowl with burning dried neem leaves (repels hostile forces).
- **West.** A whole lemon pierced with four clove buds (absorbs excess preta energy).

**3. Outer Zone — Protective Mantra Circle**

- Using rice flour mixed with turmeric, draw a full circle around the arrangement.
- On this circle, write ॐ ह्रीं क्षौं फट् at the four cardinal points.
- At each mantra point, place a til-oil lamp facing outward.

### VI.3 Operator's Position

- Sit exactly one arm-length north of the yantra, facing south.
- Keep a rudrākṣa mālā in the right hand for mantra japa.
- Left hand rests on left knee in chin-mudrā (index finger touching thumb).

### VI.4 Invocation and Dialogue Sequence

**1. Prārambha Rakṣā (Opening Protection)**
a. Close eyes, chant the Hanumān Cālīsā once, visualizing a golden aura around you.

**2. Preta Invitation**
a. Chant 108 times:

> ॐ प्रेतात्मने आवाहयाशम सत्यं प्रकािय प्रकािय स्वाहा
> *[Om Pretātmane Āvāhayāmi Satyaṁ Prakāśaya Prakāśaya Svāhā]*

b. Fix gaze on the yantra's bindu — do not look around.

**3. Communication**
a. Ask questions clearly, in short sentences.
b. Observe flame flicker, smell change, temperature drop, or auditory cues.
c. If communication is unclear, repeat the invocation 11 times.

**4. Release**
a. After receiving the cause of the haunting, chant 11 times:

> ॐ नमो भगवते रुद्ररूपिणे प्रतमक्ततदाय स्वाहा
> *[Om Namo Bhagavate Rudra-Rūpiṇe Preta-Mukti-Dāya Svāhā]*

b. Pour the water from the northern bowl into the earth (or down a drain) while visualizing the preta departing southward.

### VI.5 Closing and Disposal

- Extinguish all lamps except the central one last.
- Fold the yantra inward so the inscriptions face each other.
- Dispose by immersion in running water or burial under a peepal tree with sesame and milk.
- Bathe before sunrise and avoid sleeping until dawn — this prevents lingering contact in dreams.

## VII. The Seven Preta-Prashna (Diagnostic Question Sequence)

These are the mūla-prashna of preta-saṁvāda — the diagnostic key to know why a spirit is bound and haunting. They are crafted so the preta is drawn into truth-speaking while avoiding provocation or misdirection.

Speak them slowly, with firm but compassionate intent, always after the preta has responded to the invocation mantra. Between each question, pause for signs — flame movement, sound, temperature, scent, or inner voice.

### 1. Āgaman Kāraṇa — Cause of Arrival

> "Tum yahān kyun āye ho?"
> (Why have you come here?)

Opens the conversation. Note whether the reason is vengeance, debt, attachment, or confusion.

### 2. Bandhana Kāraṇa — Cause of Binding

> "Tum is sthān se bandhe kyon ho?"
> (Why are you bound to this place/person?)

Reveals the karmic anchor or object/person they cannot leave.

### 3. Antim Smṛti — Final Memory Before Death

> "Tumhāre mṛtyu se pahle ant mein kya ghaṭa?"
> (What happened in the moments before your death?)

Shows the trauma imprint that keeps them restless.

### 4. Ṛṇa-Pāśa — Debt Chain

> "Kya tum kisi ṛṇa (karz/obligation) se bandhe ho?"
> (Are you bound by any debt or obligation?)

Determines whether release requires ritual payment or offering.

### 5. Śatru-Bhāva — Enemy Presence

> "Kya tumhāra kisi se śatru-bhāva hai?"
> (Do you hold enmity toward someone?)

Identifies whether the haunting is targeted or random.

### 6. Āśā-Moha — Desire Attachment

> "Kya tumhe kuch chhoṛne mein kathiṇāī ho rahi hai?"
> (Is there something you cannot let go of?)

Reveals lingering earthly attachment — property, person, object.

### 7. Mukti-Mārga — Path to Release

> "Tumhe kaise mukti milegi?"
> (What will give you release?)

The most crucial — this tells the precise offering, ritual, or act needed to end the haunting.

### Signs of Truth vs. Misdirection

- **Truth response.** Steady flame, cool stillness, consistent sensory sign (same knock, same scent).
- **Misdirection / lie.** Rapid flicker, sudden heat, chaotic noises. In such case, repeat the question with the bīja-mantra ॐ ह्रं क्षौं फट् three times before asking again.

### Post-Dialogue Dharma

Once answers are recorded, the practitioner must act on the release instructions — whether it is offering food (piṇḍa-dāna), returning an object, apologizing to a living person, or performing mantra-japa for the soul. If the practitioner fails to follow through, the preta may return, stronger.

## VIII. When a Preta is Bound to an Object Held by a Living Person

If a preta is bound because its object — a possession from life, or something it was karmically tied to — is in the hands of a living person who refuses to return it, three truths must be known.

### VIII.1 Why the Preta Cannot Let Go

In the preta's consciousness, that object is not just material — it is a tether. It could be:

- **Physical anchor** (e.g. ashes, clothing, jewelry, weapon, book)
- **Ritual anchor** (yantra, idol, talisman tied to their vow)
- **Karmic anchor** (symbol of an unresolved debt or promise)

Until that tether is cut, they will circle it like a moth to a flame.

### VIII.2 If the Holder Refuses to Return It

When the living holder says "I will not return it" — there are two dharmic choices.

#### A. Karmic Substitution (Āpūrti-Kriyā)

Create an energetic replacement for the preta — an object ritually empowered to take the karmic link's place, so the preta can be released without the original.

**Process:**
1. Find an object of similar material/weight (cloth for cloth, metal for metal).
2. During the midnight rite, place it on the yantra in place of the original.
3. Chant 108 times:

> ॐ प्रेतबंिमोचनाय ह्रं क्षौं फट् स्वाहा
> *[Om Pretabandha-Mocanāya Hrīṁ Kṣauṁ Phaṭ Svāhā]*
> (Om, for the releasing of spirit-bonds, Hrīṁ Kṣauṁ Phaṭ Svāhā.)

4. Mentally declare: *"Let this stand as your own — release the other."*
5. Offer the substituted object in flowing water or at a śmaśāna (cremation ground).

#### B. Astral Severance (Bandhana-Chedana)

If no substitute works, sever the preta's emotional thread to the object.

**Process:**
1. Place a lemon at the yantra's bindu (center).
2. Visualize the preta tied to the object by a black cord.
3. With a consecrated iron blade or scissors, make a single cut through the lemon while chanting:

> ॐ छिक्न्ि बन्िनम्स्वाहा
> *[Om Chindhi Bandhanaṁ Svāhā]*
> (Om, cut the bondage, Svāhā.)

4. Discard the lemon at a crossroads without looking back.

### VIII.3 Warning About Suppressed Justice

If the object's retention is adharmic (e.g. stolen, taken by deceit) and the preta is attached through righteous grievance — even after release astrally, the karmic weight will shift to the living holder. That person will begin to experience disturbances or karmic loss in some other form, for karma does not dissolve without payment.

> **Counsel.** Always attempt Āpūrti-Kriyā first — it is the gentlest and most stable. Bandhana-Chedana is more forceful and should only be done if the preta's presence is causing severe harm and the living holder will not yield.

## IX. The Preta-Mukti Kriyā (Complete Liberation Rite)

This is the complete liberation rite performed immediately after questioning, ensuring the preta departs without lingering, even if the original cause (such as an object) cannot be returned. It combines śānti (pacification), mukti (liberation), and bandhana-chedana/āpūrti (karmic tether release).

### IX.1 Time, Place, Mind

- **Time.** Directly after the Preta-Saṁvāda (same night, without delay).
- **Place.** Same Chakra-Maṇḍala arrangement used for questioning.
- **Mind.** Firm intention: *"You shall go to your next realm free, without harm to any being."*

### IX.2 Required Materials

- The Preta-Sandhi Yantra (already in place from questioning)
- One fresh, uncut lemon
- Black sesame seeds — 21 count
- Mustard-oil lamp (already lit in center)
- Bowl of clean water (north position)
- If object-return is impossible: a substitute object similar in nature (cloth for cloth, metal for metal, etc.)

### IX.3 The Kriyā Steps

**1. Invocation of Mukti**

Chant 11 times with palms joined over the yantra:

> ॐ नमो भगवते रुद्ररूपिणे प्रतमक्ततदाय स्वाहा
> *[Om Namo Bhagavate Rudra-Rūpiṇe Preta-Mukti-Dāya Svāhā]*

Visualize the preta surrounded by cool white light, calm and receptive.

**2. Cause-Release Adaptation**

*If no object is involved:*
- Sprinkle the 21 black sesame seeds clockwise around the yantra while chanting:

> ॐ क्षौं प्रेतबंिमोचनाय स्वाहा
> *[Om Kṣauṁ Pretabandha-Mocanāya Svāhā]*

*If object is involved and can be returned later:*
- Promise aloud: *"Your belonging will be restored. Until then, be at peace."*
- Place the lemon on the yantra's bindu and proceed to step 3.

*If object cannot be returned (refusal):*

Perform **Āpūrti-Kriyā**:
1. Place the substitute object on the yantra.
2. Chant 108 times:

> ॐ प्रेतबंिमोचनाय ह्रं क्षौं फट् स्वाहा
> *[Om Pretabandha-Mocanāya Hrīṁ Kṣauṁ Phaṭ Svāhā]*

3. After the rite, immerse the substitute object in running water or leave at a śmaśāna.

If the preta is emotionally aggressive toward the object → perform **Bandhana-Chedana**:
1. Place the lemon at the bindu.
2. Visualize the preta tied to the object by a black cord.
3. Cut the lemon in one motion with a consecrated iron blade or scissors while chanting:

> ॐ छिक्न्ि बन्िनम्स्वाहा
> *[Om Chindhi Bandhanaṁ Svāhā]*

4. Discard the lemon at a crossroads without turning back.

**3. The Mukti Offering**

- Pour the northern bowl of water into a copper plate.
- Add the remaining black sesame seeds into it.
- Facing south, chant:

> ॐ पितभ्यः स्विा नमः
> *[Om Pitṛbhyaḥ Svadhā Namaḥ]*

- Offer this water to earth or flowing water — symbolic piṇḍa-dāna for the preta's soul.

**4. Closing Protection**

- Circle the yantra clockwise with the mustard-oil lamp three times, chanting: ॐ ह्रं क्षौं फट्
- Extinguish outer lamps first, central lamp last.
- Fold the yantra inward, inscriptions facing each other.
- Dispose by immersion in running water or burial under a peepal tree with sesame and milk before sunrise.

**5. Final Self-Purification**

- Bathe in lukewarm water with a pinch of rock salt.
- Recite the Hanumān Cālīsā or the Mahāmṛtyuñjaya Mantra 21 times before sleeping.

### IX.4 Important Cautions

- Do not perform this kriyā more than once a fortnight — it drains prāṇa.
- If after three days a lingering presence is sensed, repeat only the Bandhana-Chedana or Āpūrti-Kriyā step — not the full questioning again.
- Never store any leftover materials from the rite in your home.

### X.4 The 7 Preta-Prashna Sequence

1. *Tum yahān kyun āye ho?* (Why have you come?)
2. *Tum is sthān se bandhe kyon ho?* (Why are you bound to this place?)
3. *Tumhāre mṛtyu se pahle kya ghaṭa?* (What happened before your death?)
4. *Kya tum kisi ṛṇa se bandhe ho?* (Are you bound by any debt?)
5. *Kya tumhāra kisi se śatru-bhāva hai?* (Do you hold enmity toward someone?)
6. *Kya tumhe kuch chhoṛne mein kathiṇāī hai?* (Is there something you cannot let go of?)
7. *Tumhe kaise mukti milegi?* (What will give you release?)

### X.5 Liberation Steps

1. After questioning, choose No Object / Object Return / Āpūrti / Bandhana-Chedana path.
2. Perform the selected release step.
3. Pour northern bowl water + sesame to earth/river.
4. Circle yantra with lamp (3×) chanting ॐ ह्रं क्षौं फट्.
5. Extinguish lamps (outer → inner).
6. Fold yantra inward, dispose in river or bury under peepal tree with sesame and milk before sunrise.
7. Salt bath + 21× Mahāmṛtyuñjaya or Hanumān Cālīsā.

### ⚠ Notes

- Do not repeat the full rite within 15 days.
- Never keep materials used in the rite at home.
- Always act on what the preta reveals for true closure.
